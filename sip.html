What is a SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a tool that helps you create wealth, by investing small sums of money at regular intervals over a period of time. Just like a recurring deposit with a bank, you can invest a fixed sum month on month in a mutual fund scheme.

Rupee Cost Averaging

One of the biggest benefits of SIP is Rupee (or Dollar) Cost Averaging. You don't need to time the market. When the market is down, your fixed amount buys more units. When the market is up, you buy fewer units. Over time, your average cost of purchase lowers, maximizing returns.

SIP vs Lump Sum

While lump sum investments are great when you have a large amount of cash and the market is low, SIP is generally safer and more disciplined for most investors. It removes the emotional element from investing.

Magic of Compounding in SIP

The longer you stay invested, the more your money grows. A small monthly contribution of $500 at 12% for 10 years grows to ~$116,000. But if you continue for another 10 years, it grows to ~$499,000! That is the power of time and compounding.
