The Power of Compound Interest

Albert Einstein famously called compound interest the "eighth wonder of the world." Unlike simple interest, where you earn interest only on your principal, compound interest allows you to earn interest on your interest.

How It Works

Imagine you invest $1000 at 10% annual interest.

  • Year 1: You earn $100. Total = $1100.
  • Year 2: You earn 10% on $1100, which is $110. Total = $1210.
  • Year 3: You earn 10% on $1210, which is $121. Total = $1331.

Over time, this effect accelerates, leading to exponential growth of your wealth.

The Rule of 72

A quick way to estimate compound interest is the Rule of 72. Divide 72 by your annual interest rate to see how many years it will take to double your money.

For example, at an 8% return, your money will double in approximately 9 years (72 / 8 = 9).

How to Maximize Returns

  1. Start Early: Time is the most critical component. The longer your money compounds, the more you make.
  2. Increase Contributions: Regularly adding to your principal (like in a SIP) accelerates growth.
  3. Reinvest Dividends: Always reinvest your gains to keep the compounding cycle going.
